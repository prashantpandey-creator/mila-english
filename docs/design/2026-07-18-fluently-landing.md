# Landing direction study — 2026-07-18

## References

- [Fluently](https://getfluently.app/) — full-bleed opening image, one blunt
  promise, immediate speaking CTA, product UI as the proof, then large editorial
  sections. Strongest reference for hierarchy and confidence.
- [Speak](https://www.speak.com/) — human promise first, a single free-speaking
  action, and a simple learn/practice/apply narrative. Strongest reference for
  emotional clarity.
- [ELSA Speak](https://elsaspeak.com/en/) — proof and product claims close to the
  first action, with explicit audience/use-case structure. Strongest reference
  for conversion clarity, but too conventional as a visual model for Mila.

## Three worlds considered

1. **Conversation poster** — commissioned room at full scale, editorial type,
   one speaking action, solid proof band. No glass, glow, carousel, or duplicate
   intro. This is the selected direction.
2. **Warm editorial paper** — image-free cream front page with a large quote and
   a phone-shaped product demo. Rejected for the front screen: safe, but it hides
   the emotional value of Mila's existing artwork.
3. **Studio signal** — black console, mono labels, waveform and pronunciation
   data above the fold. Rejected for the welcome room: useful for practice
   surfaces, but too technical and heavy for a first meeting with Mila.

## Translation into Mila

- Keep Mila's own language: warm, private, conversational, and available in
  Russia without a VPN.
- Use the cinematic headphones scene as atmosphere, not a thumbnail gallery.
- Merge the two old hero sections into one promise: “Speak freely. Sound like
  you.” / “Говори свободно. Оставайся собой.”
- Make **Talk with Mila** the obvious first action; keep the level check as a
  secondary route.
- Follow the hero with three plain outcomes, then show the pronunciation UI on
  a solid Studio surface.
- Keep the assistant clear of the proof band at desktop and verify both English
  and Russian at mobile width.
